How to use MotoTracks

Your personal ride visualizer — everything runs in your browser
📂
Importing your tracks
1
Export a ride as a .gpx file from your recording app (OsmAnd, Komoot, Strava, Garmin Connect etc.)
2
Drag & drop the file onto the import zone in the sidebar — or click it to browse your device. You can drop multiple files at once.
3
Your tracks appear on the map instantly. The app remembers them next time you open it — no need to re-import.
Tip: You can also drop a .zip backup file directly — the app unpacks it and restores all your tracks automatically.
🗺️
Using the map
1
Hover over a track to see its name, date and distance in a tooltip.
2
Click a track on the map or in the sidebar list to zoom the map to that ride.
3
Use Fit all in the sidebar to zoom out and see all visible tracks at once.
🎨
Managing tracks
1
Click the colour dot next to any track to open the colour picker — choose a preset or pick any custom colour.
2
Click the 👁 eye icon to hide or show a track without deleting it.
3
Hover over a track row and click ✕ to permanently delete it from the app and storage.
🔍
Filtering
1
Use the Year and Month dropdowns to show only tracks from a specific period.
2
The Country filter is filled automatically — the app detects which country each ride is in.
3
Use Search name to quickly find a specific ride by name. Hit Reset filters to clear everything.
Note: Country detection uses your internet connection to look up each ride's location. It may take a few seconds after import.
💾
Backup & restore
1
Click Export ZIP in the header to download a complete backup of all your tracks, including your custom colours.
2
Store the zip in Google Drive, iCloud or any folder you trust — it's your safety net if you ever clear your browser.
3
To restore, just drop the zip file into the import zone. New tracks added since the backup will be merged in.
Good habit: Export a new zip every few tracks. The file grows as you add more trips — it's always a full snapshot.
📸
Sharing your tracks
1
Use Fit all in the sidebar to frame all your visible tracks on the map — this becomes your share image.
2
Click Share in the header to open the share panel, then hit Generate preview to render the image.
3
Toggle Include stats overlay on or off — the overlay shows your total km, tracks and countries in an Endurious-branded card on the image.
4
Hit Download image to save it as a PNG — ready to share on Instagram, WhatsApp or wherever you like.
Tip: You can also use filters first to show only certain tracks — for example just one country or one year — then hit Fit all and Share for a focused shot.
1
Click Statistics in the header to open your personal riding stats panel.
2
See total km, number of tracks, countries visited, longest and shortest track, most active year, and a breakdown by month.
